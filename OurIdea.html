Bonded labor is a pervasive problem in the Indian brick industry, where workers are forced to work in inhumane conditions with little or no pay, and are unable to leave due to their indebtedness to their employers. This problem affects a significant number of people, particularly those from marginalized communities, and violates their basic human rights. The problem of bonded labor in the Indian brick industry has been widely recognized by human rights organizations, but effective solutions to eradicate this practice have yet to be implemented. Therefore, there is a pressing need to address this issue and develop strategies to end bonded labor in the Indian brick industry.
